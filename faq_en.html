FAQ

1. I don't know my level.

I strongly recommend to fill out my test by clicking here.

2. What's the password for signing up to courses

I need to make sure that all participants of my courses are at the required level to guarantee that everyone gets the most out of their learning. To sign up to my courses first you need to take a test to check your level. You can access the test by clicking here.

After I checked your results, I will send you the password with which you can sign up to all courses that suit your level.

3. How many students are in a group lesson?

You can see the minimum and the maximum number of participants of each lesson at the end of the description of the lesson/course. The maximum number ensures that groups are small and each participant can speak and practice English in a useful manner.

4. What does the "minimum number of participants" mean in the lesson/course descriptions?

If less participants sign up than this minimum number, then the lesson/course will be cancelled. In this case everyone who signed up will be fully reimbursed.

It is likely that the lesson/course will be announced again at a later time. If you want to be notified of this, sign up to the newsletter!

5. What can I expect from the newsletter?

I will regularly announce new lessons, workshops and courses in the newsletter. If you want to know about them before the spaces fill up, sign up for the newsletter.

Occasionally, I will also send exclusive discount codes to newsletter members.

I may also share some learning tips and links to useful resources to help you make the most out of your studies.

6. Why is the price of the course higher today than yesterday?

For most lessons, workshops and courses I offer an early-bird price which is a discounted price for those who sign up early. This lower price is valid usually until 1 week before the first lesson in the series/course.

You can see both the discounted (if still valid) and the full prices on the lesson/course description page, high up close to the title. The end of the discount period is also shown as "Before 1 June 2023".

Why do I have two prices? Because it generates a win-win situation. You get the lesson/course cheaper, and I get a better idea early on on how many students I can expect, so I can prepare better for the course. Also, I will see in time if no one is interested in a specific course at the moment and I will have to cancel it. So don't wait for long with signing up to workshops and courses you like because you can get a better experience for a lower cost if you are one of the early birds.
